Classrooms

We encourage academic rigour and intellectual curiosity.

Our School is proudly affiliated with the CBSE, ensuring a world-class education for all our students. We take pride in offering a curriculum that is taught in English, and supported by Hindi. Our focus is to provide a well-rounded education that caters to the needs of every student. Sanskrit/ French are a third choice from classes VI to VIII.

We have shown excellent results in both Class X & XII Board examinations. A sizable number of our students are subject toppers for the National Capital Region.

Learning to be, learning to know, learning to live, learning to learn.

EXERCISE IN MINDFULNESS

Students are made to feel connected to everything that goes on in the world - through projects, curriculum related activities like excursions, workshops, lectures, seminars, films and national and international festivals and anniversaries. Teacher and student workshops are held by eminent subject experts. Counsellors and teachers work with parents to guide students in identifying their capabilities and potential. Classes IX, X, XI and XII are given special counselling. In classes XI and XII three streams are offered – namely Commerce, Science and Humanities. The syllabus for classes I to XII is according to the latest guidelines laid down by CBSE, the NCERT and NEP 2020. Physical Education and Work Experience are an essential part of the curriculum. Computer Science is compulsory for classes IV to X.

Bagless days for classes VI to VIII on Saturdays.

MAKING MODELS, BIOLOGY LAB

We encourage independence in our students to develop individual interests and be passionate about what they wish to do.

In Junior School, the curriculum is based on the fundamentals – mathematics, environmental science, language and reasoning – the basics of all learning. The assessment is conducted in accordance with the guidelines provided by CBSE.

The Middle and Senior School curriculum is synchronised with CBSE. Assessments are conducted according to guidelines provided by CBSE. Subjects at the senior level offer learners a plethora of choices that appeal to their varying areas of interest.

DRAMA IS A PART OF LEARNING

A wide array of activities are in place, keeping in mind the holistic development of students, shaping them into well rounded individuals. To stimulate creativity and critical thinking in students, drama plays a pivotal role in the education process. By incorporating drama into education, students experience a multitude of positive outcomes, enhancing their skills and cultivating an engaging learning environment.

Drama is a part of learning at The Indian School, and students learn interpersonal, collaborative, and public speaking skills through drama. Nukkad Nataks are performed by students on social issues like gender equality, corruption and caste system. The Urdu storytelling art form Dastangoi, has provided students a platform to enhance their storytelling skills.
